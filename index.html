Black forest cake is a renowned German chocolate cake, featuring 3 layers of chocolate genoise stuffed with morello cherries and covered in a light whipped cream icing. Traditionally prepared with kirsch-soaked cake layers, the cake in this recipe can be enjoyed by all ages sans alcohol.

  • 2 1/8 cups all-purpose flour

  • 2 cups white sugar

  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder

  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder

  • 3/4 teaspoon baking soda

  • 3/4 teaspoon salt

  • 3 eggs

  • 1 cup milk

  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil

  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

  • 2 (20 ounce) cans pitted morello cherries

  • 1 cup white sugar

  • 1/4 cup cornstarch

  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • 3 cups heavy whipping cream

  • 1/3 cup confectioners' sugar

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour three 8 inch round cake pans; cover bottoms with waxed paper.

  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, 2 cups sugar, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Add eggs, milk, oil, and 1 tablespoon vanilla; beat until well blended. Pour batter into prepared pans.

  3. Bake for 35 minutes, or until wooden toothpick inserted in centers comes out clean. Cool layers in pans on wire racks 10 minutes. Remove from pans and let cool completely.

  4. Drain cherries, reserving 1/2 cup juice. Combine reserved juice, cherries, 1 cup sugar and cornstarch in a 2 quart saucepan. Cook over low heat until thickened, stirring constantly. Stir in 1 teaspoon vanilla. Cool before using.

  5. Combine whipping cream and confectioner's sugar in a chilled medium bowl. Beat with an electric mixer at high speed until stiff peaks form.

  6. To assemble, place one cake layer on cake plate. Spread with 1 cup frosting; top with 3/4 cup cherry topping. Top with second cake layer and repeat. Top with third cake layer. Frost side of cake. Add chocolate glaze, fresh cherries, etc. for decoration as desired.
